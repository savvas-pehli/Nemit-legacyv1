About the Network of Stations

According to national and EU legislation, it is the country's obligation to operate a network of atmospheric pollution monitoring stations. (Click here for Metadata Stations)

For this purpose, and using resources from the 2nd Community Support Framework (CSF) within the framework of the Operational Programme "ENVIRONMENT," the Ministry of Environment and Energy (YPEN), then known as the Ministry for the Environment, Physical Planning and Public Works (YPEHODE), upgraded existing stations in the Athens basin and other cities and also installed new stations in major cities, thus creating the National Air Pollution Monitoring Network (EDPAR). EDPAR began operations at the end of 2000.

In 2015, using resources from the NSRF 2007–2013 under the Operational Programme for Environment and Sustainable Development (EPPERAA), EDPAR stations operated by YPEN were upgraded with new air pollution analyzers, certified according to European standards. Additionally, the number of monitoring locations for particulate matter (PM10 and PM2.5) and benzene was increased.

The Air Quality Department, which is part of the Directorate of Climate Change and Air Quality (KAPA) within YPEN, is responsible for operating the air pollution monitoring network in the Attica region. When EDPAR was initially launched, the responsibility for station operation (excluding the Attica region) lay with the respective regional authorities, in accordance with Law 2647/1998 (Government Gazette 237/A). Specifically, for the stations in Volos (one of the two stations) and Larissa, the corresponding Prefectural Administrations were responsible for their operation until 2010. With the implementation of the “Kallikratis” Programme (Law 3852/2010, Government Gazette 87/A) and Ministerial Decision ΗΠ 14122/549/E.103/2011 (Government Gazette 488/B), the responsibilities related to the installation and operation of air quality monitoring stations were transferred from the Decentralized Administrations to the Regional Authorities of the country.

Through the aforementioned YPEN project, the EDPAR network was renewed with new PM10 analyzers that replaced the old ones (September 2016), and it was expanded to include pollutants that had not been previously monitored, such as PM2.5 (September 2016) and benzene (September–October 2015). Additionally, a benzene analyzer was installed at a new location (Kavala). The station in Kavala, operated by the Regional Unit of Kavala, was relocated in 2019.

From 2020 to 2022, a second station operated in Volos under the Region of Thessaly. Since 2023, the Region of Thessaly has installed and operates two additional stations in Larissa and Volos, and one each in Trikala and Karditsa. It is noted that stations in Ioannina were installed in 2008 and 2019 by the local Regional Authority. Since 2016, the Region of Central Greece has operated a network of PM10 monitoring stations in Lamia, Livadia, Chalkida, Amfissa, and Karpenisi. Since 2017, the Region of Crete has carried out indicative PM10 sampling in Chania, in addition to the existing EDPAR station in Heraklion. These stations ceased operation in 2022, when four new stations began operating, along with the renewal of the background station of the University of Crete in Finokalia. The Region of Western Greece has operated a new station in Agrinio since 2019. Since 2020, the Region of Western Macedonia has operated four stations in the Regional Unit of Kozani, and since 2023, one station each in Kastoria and Grevena. The Region of Central Macedonia has operated three new stations in Thessaloniki since 2022.
